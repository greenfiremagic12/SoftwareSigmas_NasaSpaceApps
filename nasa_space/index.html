Harnessing NASA Earth data to design a sustainable, equitable, and resilient New York City

We combine NASA Earth observation with NYC open data to identify underserved neighborhoods, heat-vulnerable communities, and waste management opportunities.

Explore the Dashboard See Focus Areas

About the Challenge

This project addresses NASA Space Apps’ “Data Pathways to Healthy Cities and Human Settlements” theme using New York City as a case study. We analyze land use, temperature, and waste data to propose actionable urban planning interventions.

Focus Areas

Access to Food, Housing & Transportation

Map food deserts, transit gaps, and housing stress to recommend targeted improvements.

Public Health & Climate Resilience

Detect urban heat islands and air quality hotspots to prioritize cooling and health resources.

Waste Management & Pollution

Monitor landfill growth and optimize collection routes to reduce emissions and costs.

Data & Methods

  • Satellite: Landsat, MODIS, VIIRS (surface temp, land cover, nighttime lights)
  • Local data: NYC Open Data (transit, health, sanitation)
  • Tools: Google Earth Engine, QGIS, Python, Leaflet
