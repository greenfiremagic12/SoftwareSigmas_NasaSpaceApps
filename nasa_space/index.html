Focus Areas — Building a Resilient, Healthy New York

Our project targets three interconnected urban challenges using NASA Earth observation and NYC Open Data.

🥦 Food Access

Challenge: Thousands of New Yorkers live in “food deserts” with limited access to affordable, nutritious food.

Data: NYC Food Access dataset combined with NASA POWER temperature and solar data.

Goal: Identify overlap between heat stress and poor food access to guide cooling and food security initiatives.

🌡️ Urban Heat Islands

Challenge: Dense infrastructure creates localized heat zones, worsening health risks during summer.

Data: NASA MODIS thermal imagery and NYC Heat Vulnerability Index zones.

Goal: Support equitable climate resilience by highlighting “hot spots” needing cooling centers and green spaces.

🗑️ Waste Management

Challenge: Waste transfer stations are concentrated near low-income neighborhoods.

Data: NYC Waste Facility dataset integrated with NASA environmental indicators.

Goal: Promote fairer zoning and circular economy planning through data transparency.

Data & Methods — How We Built Smart New York

We combined open-source datasets and satellite observations to visualize NYC’s environmental and social landscape.

1️⃣ Data Sources

  • NASA POWER API: Daily surface temperature and solar irradiance data.
  • NASA MODIS / Terra Imagery: Land surface temperature and vegetation indices.
  • NYC Open Data: Food Access, Heat Vulnerability, and Waste Management datasets.

2️⃣ Data Integration & Visualization

Data was converted to GeoJSON for mapping in Leaflet.js and charting in Plotly.js. We overlaid NASA datasets with NYC layers to identify correlations between heat, food, and waste patterns.

3️⃣ Analytical Insights

  • Mapped overlap between high-heat zones and food deserts.
  • Visualized waste site density in heat-vulnerable regions.
  • Created interactive NASA-powered visualizations for public insight.

4️⃣ Tools & Frameworks

Our stack integrates modern web technologies and open APIs:

  • 🌍 Leaflet.js — Interactive mapping
  • 📈 Plotly.js — Data visualization
  • 🛰️ NASA POWER & MODIS APIs — Satellite observations
  • 🏙️ NYC Open Data — Urban datasets
  • 💻 Bootstrap / HTML / CSS / JS — Responsive design

Outcome: This integration of NASA science with city data offers decision-makers a unified dashboard to identify inequities and plan resilient, sustainable interventions.
